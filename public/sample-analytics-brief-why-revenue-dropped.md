# Sample Analytics Brief: Why Revenue Dropped This Week

## Executive Summary

Revenue declined 14.8% week over week, mainly because conversion rate fell in mobile traffic from paid social campaigns. Traffic volume stayed broadly stable, but fewer sessions converted into orders. The highest-impact segment was new mobile users from Meta campaigns landing on product-detail pages.

Recommended action: audit the paid-social landing flow, pause the weakest creative/ad set combinations, and run a focused checkout-friction review on mobile product pages.

## KPI Movement

| Metric | Previous Week | Current Week | Change |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Revenue | GBP 118,400 | GBP 100,900 | -14.8% |
| Sessions | 82,100 | 80,700 | -1.7% |
| Orders | 2,480 | 2,060 | -16.9% |
| Conversion Rate | 3.02% | 2.55% | -15.6% |
| Average Order Value | GBP 47.74 | GBP 48.98 | +2.6% |

## Likely Drivers

1. Mobile conversion rate dropped from 2.71% to 2.12%, accounting for most of the revenue loss.
2. Paid social traffic had stable sessions but lower purchase completion, especially among new users.
3. Returning customers and email traffic remained healthy, which reduces the likelihood of a sitewide pricing or inventory issue.
4. AOV rose slightly, so the revenue drop is not caused by discounting or lower basket size.

## Business Recommendation

Start with the paid-social mobile flow because it has the highest revenue impact and the clearest segment-level signal. Do not redesign the full storefront yet. The evidence points to a narrower acquisition or landing-flow issue, not a global business decline.

## Next 48 Hours

- Pull campaign, creative, landing page, and device-level performance.
- Review mobile landing pages for page speed, broken variants, checkout errors, and CTA visibility.
- Pause bottom-quartile Meta ad sets until conversion rate recovers.
- Create a daily anomaly alert for conversion rate by channel, device, and user type.
